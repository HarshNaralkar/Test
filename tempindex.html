Overview

The Department of Mathematics, established in 1946, offers an undergraduate program with around 620 students and five faculty members. It follows a three-year unitized syllabus (FYBSc, SYBSc, TYBSc). The department hosts extracurricular activities, including a yearly Mathematical Festival on National Mathematics Day, celebrating Ramanujan, with competitions and games. Guest lectures and short-term courses are also organized. Under NEP 2020, students gain opportunities in fields like banking, data science, and teaching. Students frequently attend MTTS programs, and one recently won second prize in a state-level seminar competition. The department also regularly updates its library with relevant books.

Our Objective

To advance the frontiers of mathematical knowledge while preparing students for leadership roles in academia, industry, and government. We emphasize critical thinking, problem-solving, and computational skills, ensuring a well-rounded mathematical education.
